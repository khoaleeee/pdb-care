Northern Virginian Daily

August 5, 2024 — "A Shower for the Mind"

Gray skies, cold temps, low light: a recipe for SAD. Dr. Sophia Wu, a neurologist and neuroscientist, notes that patients often report sadness, fatigue, increased sleep, and cravings for high-calorie foods during colder months. Studies have shown students tend to have lower grades during winter seasons.
